Is JFK Airport Open 24 Hours?

Yes, JFK airport is open 24 hours. John F. Kennedy International Airport operates continuously, processing early morning departures, late-night arrivals, and red-eye flights throughout the night. As one of the primary U.S. gateways for transatlantic routes, JFK handles significant overnight international traffic.

Is JFK open 24 hours for sleeping? Yes. Travelers may remain in the post-security gate areas overnight. Security checkpoints do not require travelers to exit the terminal after hours.

Does JFK Have Sleeping Pods?

Yes, JFK airport has sleeping pods. Minute Suites are available at JFK Terminal 4, providing private pay-per-hour rest rooms inside the secured terminal area. This makes Terminal 4 the best terminal at JFK for paid in-terminal sleeping.

Does JFK airport have sleeping pods in other terminals? Minute Suites at JFK are specifically in Terminal 4. Terminals 1, 2, 5, and 8 do not currently have dedicated commercial sleep pod facilities.

Sleep Pods JFK Terminal 4: Minute Suites

Minute Suites JFK are located in Terminal 4, which is the largest terminal at John F. Kennedy International Airport and handles a wide range of international and domestic carriers including Delta, Air France, Korean Air, and many others.

What Is Included at JFK Minute Suites?

Each JFK sleeping room via Minute Suites includes:

  • A full-size daybed with fresh linens
  • 40-inch TV with streaming capability
  • Bose noise-masking audio system
  • Individual climate control
  • High-speed Wi-Fi
  • Dedicated power outlets and USB charging
  • Private, locking door for security

The soundproofing and noise-masking system are particularly valuable at JFK Terminal 4, which maintains high overnight activity due to numerous international arrivals and early morning departures.

Minute Suites JFK Location

Minute Suites at JFK are located on the upper level of Terminal 4, inside the post-security area. Look for signage near the main food court level in T4.

Pricing for Sleep Pods at JFK Terminal 4

JFK airport sleep pods via Minute Suites follow standard pricing:

  • Hourly rate: Approximately $45 to $55
  • Minimum booking: 1 hour
  • 8-hour cap rate: Available; verify current pricing directly
  • Additional guest: Permitted for a small fee

Advance booking via the Minute Suites website or app is recommended, especially during peak international travel seasons.

JFK Airport Showers

JFK airport showers are available through several lounge facilities in Terminal 4 and other terminals.

Delta Sky Club Showers (Terminal 4)

Delta's primary Sky Club in Terminal 4 offers shower facilities to Sky Club members and qualifying Delta premium ticket holders. The shower suites at the T4 Sky Club are well-maintained and a highly sought-after amenity for long-haul travelers.

Korean Air and Asiana Lounges (Terminal 1)

Several international carrier lounges in Terminal 1 offer shower access to their premium class passengers.

Priority Pass Lounges with Showers at JFK

Some Priority Pass-accessible lounges in Terminal 4 offer shower access. Verify with the specific lounge for current availability.

JFK 24 Hour Lounge Options

Finding a true JFK 24 hour lounge or JFK overnight lounge requires some research, as lounge hours vary.

Delta Sky Club T4: Extended Hours

The Delta Sky Club in Terminal 4 has extended hours to accommodate international passengers, but it is not strictly 24 hours. Hours vary by specific location within T4.

American Express Centurion Lounge at JFK

The Centurion Lounge at JFK (Terminal 4) is one of the most comprehensive lounge options at the airport. Access requires an eligible American Express card. Hours are not 24/7.

For True 24-Hour Rest at JFK

The most reliable 24-hour paid sleep option at JFK remains Minute Suites in Terminal 4. Unlike lounges with operating hour restrictions, Minute Suites operate continuously and are specifically designed for overnight use.

Best Free Places to Sleep at JFK

For travelers looking for places to sleep in JFK at no cost, here are the viable options:

Terminal 4 Gate Areas

T4 is the largest terminal and has the most seating. The gate areas away from the main central hub tend to be quieter overnight. Look for gates in the B or C concourse sections of T4 that have later last-departure times.

Terminal 5 (JetBlue)

Terminal 5 handles JetBlue and has comfortable, relatively modern seating. The adjacency to the TWA Hotel means 24-hour food options are accessible. T5 is among the more comfortable free sleeping terminals at JFK.

Terminal 8 (American Airlines)

Terminal 8 has seating throughout the gate areas. Activity picks up early for first American Banks departures around 5 a.m., so expect noise from approximately 4:30 a.m.

What to Know About Free Sleeping at JFK

JFK does not restrict overnight sleeping in terminal seating. However, the seating design in newer terminal sections uses fixed armrests that prevent lying across multiple seats. Bring a travel pillow and be prepared to sleep seated if lying down is not possible.

Tips for Sleeping at JFK Airport

  1. Target Terminal 4 for the best paid and free options. T4 has Minute Suites, a Delta Sky Club, the Centurion Lounge, and extensive gate seating.
  2. The AirTrain runs 24 hours between all terminals. You can move between terminals overnight, giving you flexibility to seek quieter gate areas.
  3. Set multiple alarms. T4 is a large terminal. Walking from Minute Suites to your gate can take 10 or more minutes depending on which gate your flight departs from.
  4. JFK security can have long lines even at 4 a.m. If you are using a hotel near JFK (outside the secured area), budget extra time for re-entry through security.
  5. The Delta Sky Club is the most consistently reviewed comfortable rest option at JFK. If you have Sky Club access, the T4 location is a reliable place to rest overnight.
