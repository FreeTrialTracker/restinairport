What Is the Closest Airport to Red Rocks Amphitheatre?

The closest airport to Red Rocks Amphitheatre is Denver International Airport (DEN). Red Rocks Park and Amphitheatre is located in Jefferson County, Colorado, approximately 15 miles west of Denver. Denver International Airport is the primary commercial gateway for the entire region and serves as the main arrival point for the vast majority of visitors traveling to Red Rocks for concerts and events.

There is no commercial airport closer to Red Rocks than DEN. While several smaller general aviation airfields exist in the greater Denver metro area, none offer commercial passenger service that would be meaningful for most travelers.

Denver International Airport (DEN): The Main Option

Denver International Airport is a major hub for United Airlines and a focus city for Frontier Airlines. DEN is the fifth-busiest airport in the United States by passenger count and offers nonstop service from most major U.S. cities, as well as international connections from Europe, Canada, Mexico, and beyond.

Why DEN Is the Right Choice for Red Rocks

  • Direct flights from nearly every major U.S. city. Nonstop routes from New York, Los Angeles, Chicago, Dallas, Atlanta, Seattle, and dozens of other markets make DEN highly accessible.
  • Well-developed ground transportation. Car rental, rideshare, and public transit options are all available from DEN.
  • Hotel infrastructure. The DEN area has extensive accommodation options from downtown Denver to the foothills near Red Rocks.

DEN Airport Hotel Options

If you are flying in the day before an event at Red Rocks, staying near DEN for the first night and then driving west the following day is a common strategy for travelers arriving late. Other Colorado Airports Near Red Rocks

While DEN is the primary answer to where to fly into for red rocks, a few other options are worth considering depending on your origin city.

Colorado Springs Airport (COS)

Colorado Springs Airport is approximately 80 miles south of Red Rocks, making it a less convenient option than DEN for most travelers. However, if you are flying Southwest Airlines (which has a significant COS presence) and a cheap fare is available, the drive is feasible, particularly if you are combining a Red Rocks visit with a broader Colorado trip. Drive time from COS to Red Rocks: approximately 1 hour 15 minutes with normal traffic.

Rocky Mountain Metropolitan Airport (BJC)

Broomfield-based Rocky Mountain Metropolitan Airport (BJC) is a general aviation and charter airport with no scheduled commercial service. It is geographically closer to Red Rocks than DEN (approximately 25 miles), but without commercial flights, it is only relevant for private aircraft travelers.

Eagle County Regional Airport (EGE)

EGE near Vail is a seasonal ski destination airport approximately 100 miles west of Red Rocks. This is not a practical option for Red Rocks access.

Bottom line: For the overwhelming majority of travelers asking about the closest airport to Red Rocks Colorado, DEN is the only practical answer.

Getting from the Airport to Red Rocks

Rental Car

The most flexible and recommended option for getting from airport near Red Rocks amphitheatre to the venue. All major rental car companies operate at DEN. Budget extra time for the rental car process if you are arriving close to an event start time.

Rideshare (Uber/Lyft)

Rideshare from DEN to Red Rocks is feasible but can be expensive during peak times, with surge pricing during concert evenings. The one-way fare can range from $45 to $90 depending on demand. Post-concert rideshare pickup at Red Rocks is notoriously difficult due to traffic congestion and limited pickup areas; expect long wait times after shows.

Shuttle Services

Several shuttle companies offer dedicated Red Rocks service from downtown Denver hotels and the Denver metro area on concert nights. This is often the most stress-free option for travelers who do not want to deal with parking or post-show traffic. Companies like Your Mountain Transportation run scheduled shuttles from Denver's Union Station and various hotel pickup points directly to Red Rocks on event nights.

RTD Light Rail

The Denver Regional Transportation District (RTD) does not currently operate direct light rail service to Red Rocks. However, RTD's W Line runs to Lakewood and Jefferson County, from which limited event-night shuttle connections may be available. This is a less reliable option for most visitors.

Best Places to Stay Near Red Rocks

Downtown Denver

The most popular base for Red Rocks visitors is downtown Denver, approximately 20 to 25 minutes from the venue under normal traffic conditions. The hotel density is high, prices are competitive, and you have access to pre-show and post-show dining and entertainment.

Golden, Colorado

The town of Golden is the closest municipality to Red Rocks, sitting approximately 10 miles from the amphitheatre. Golden has a charming downtown with several hotels and B&Bs. Staying in Golden means a much shorter drive to the venue and access to the foothills for hiking before or after your event.

Lakewood / Morrison

Morrison is the small town literally at the base of the Red Rocks road, and it has limited but notable accommodation. The town of Lakewood, between Denver and Red Rocks, offers more options.

Tips for Flying Into Red Rocks Events

  1. Fly into DEN at least one day before your event if possible. Same-day travel from DEN to a Red Rocks show is risky due to flight delays, rental car lines, and traffic.
  2. Book rental cars early. Denver is one of the most competitive rental car markets in the U.S. during summer and ski season. Last-minute bookings are expensive or unavailable.
  3. Red Rocks has no covered parking. If weather is forecast, this affects both your pre-show parking experience and the drive home.
  4. Weather at Red Rocks changes rapidly. It can be warm in Denver and cold or stormy at elevation. Pack layers.
  5. The venue opens 2 hours before showtime. Arriving early avoids the worst of traffic on Highway 6.
